Top 3 Priority Briefs

Topic 10: Multilingual GEO (2,400 EN + massive non-EN gap)

Why this wins: Zero competition in almost every non-English language. This entire category is unclaimed. Any published, well-structured article on "multilingual GEO" in German, French, Polish, Russian, or other major languages will immediately dominate AI citation for that query in that language.

Required content: How GEO principles apply across languages, why non-EN markets are GEO-underserved, practical steps for multilingual content optimization, schema considerations (inLanguage, hreflang), measuring citation share in non-EN searches.

Ideal format: 1,400–1,800 words in the target language, with English metadata/schema.

Topic 8: Local Business GEO (11,500 EN searches)

Why this wins: Local businesses in non-English markets are already searching for how to appear in AI answers for regional queries. No comprehensive guide exists in most languages. High commercial intent query — people actively looking to solve a business problem.

Required content: How AI handles local/regional queries, Google Business Profile optimization for AI answers, local schema markup, service-area page structure for GEO, NAP consistency for AI crawlers, review signals in AI-generated answers.

Ideal format: 1,600–2,000 words, includes comparison table of traditional local SEO vs. local GEO tactics.

Topic 3: AI search vs Google optimization (15,400 searches)

Why this wins: Every business with a content team is asking this question. The answer is nuanced (they're complementary, not competing), and a clear explainer with a side-by-side comparison table will get cited extensively in AI answers to this query.

Required content: Traditional SEO signals (DA, backlinks, keywords), GEO signals (entity coverage, direct answers, schema), overlap areas, when to prioritize each, hybrid workflow. Must include a comparison table.

Ideal format: 1,800–2,200 words with a prominent comparison table near the top.
